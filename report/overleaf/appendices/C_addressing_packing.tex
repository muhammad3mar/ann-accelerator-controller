\chapter{Addressing and Packing Conventions}
\label{app:addressing}

This appendix consolidates every bit-level convention used by the PI,
the controller, and the input buffer so that integrators can cross-check
any intermediate value in a waveform against its canonical position.

\section{16-bit Parallel Address}
\label{app:addr-16}

The 16-bit \signame{address} produced by \modname{parallel\_interface}
and consumed by \modname{ann\_controller} is

\[
    \signame{address} =
    \{\,\underbrace{6'b000000}_{\text{reserved}},\,
       \underbrace{\texttt{blk}[1{:}0]}_{\text{[9:8]}},\,
       \underbrace{\texttt{sb}[1{:}0]}_{\text{[7:6]}},\,
       \underbrace{\texttt{cid}[2{:}0]}_{\text{[5:3]}},\,
       \underbrace{\texttt{rid}[2{:}0]}_{\text{[2:0]}}\,\}
\]

Bits \sv{[15:10]} are \emph{not consumed} by \sv{parse\_ann\_address}.
The same layout is reproduced in pictorial form by
\Cref{fig:parallel-addr-layout}.

\section{32-bit \signame{ann\_core\_word}}
\label{app:word-32}

The \signame{ann\_core\_word} output of the controller is

\[
    \signame{ann\_core\_word} =
    \{\,\underbrace{\texttt{data\_byte}[7{:}0]}_{\text{[31:24]}},\,
       \underbrace{\texttt{PE}_{\text{onehot}}[3{:}0]}_{\text{[23:20]}},\,
       \underbrace{\texttt{SA}_{\text{onehot}}[3{:}0]}_{\text{[19:16]}},\,
       \underbrace{\texttt{col}_{\text{onehot}}[7{:}0]}_{\text{[15:8]}},\,
       \underbrace{\texttt{row}_{\text{onehot}}[7{:}0]}_{\text{[7:0]}}\,\}
\]

In programming contexts, the quantized weight nibble occupies the
upper half of the payload byte, i.e.\ bits \sv{[27:24]} of the word.

\section{One-Hot Predicates}
\label{app:addr-onehot}

\sv{pi\_is\_onehot4(oh)} returns 1 only for the four patterns listed
in the left half of \Cref{tab:onehot-predicates};
\sv{pi\_is\_onehot8(oh)} returns 1 only for the eight patterns listed
in the right half. Any other pattern (including all-zeros and
multi-hot) returns 0. Since \signame{valid} at the PI boundary is the
AND of all four per-field predicates, an invalid tail cannot produce
\signame{valid}~=~1.

    \centering
    \caption{Accepted patterns for \sv{pi\_is\_onehot4} and

    \label{tab:onehot-predicates}
    \begin{tabular}{l l @{\hspace{2em}} l l}
        \toprule
        \multicolumn{2}{c}{\sv{pi\_is\_onehot4} (4 patterns)} &
        \multicolumn{2}{c}{\sv{pi\_is\_onehot8} (8 patterns)} \\
        \cmidrule(r){1-2}\cmidrule(l){3-4}
        Pattern & Index & Pattern & Index \\
        \midrule
        \sv{4'b0001} & 0 & \sv{8'b00000001} & 0 \\
        \sv{4'b0010} & 1 & \sv{8'b00000010} & 1 \\
        \sv{4'b0100} & 2 & \sv{8'b00000100} & 2 \\
        \sv{4'b1000} & 3 & \sv{8'b00001000} & 3 \\
                     &   & \sv{8'b00010000} & 4 \\
                     &   & \sv{8'b00100000} & 5 \\

\section{Address Round Trip}
\label{app:addr-roundtrip}

\Cref{fig:app-address-roundtrip} depicts the complete round trip that
every host transaction goes through.

\begin{figure}[htbp]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.95\linewidth]{fig_app_address_roundtrip}
    \caption[Address round trip]{Round trip of a cell address through
        the controller stack. (1) Host provides a 32-bit
        \signame{host\_data} whose low 24 bits are one-hot
        \{PE, SA, col, row\}. (2) PI computes
        \signame{address}~=~\sv{ann\_tail\_to\_parallel\_addr(host\_data[23:0])}
        into the packed 16-bit form. (3) Controller captures
        \signame{address\_reg} on idle exit. (4) \sv{parse\_ann\_address}
        slices \signame{address\_reg} into per-field indices.
        (5) \sv{pack\_ann\_core\_word} reconstructs the 24-bit one-hot
        tail via left-shift (\sv{(1 << idx)}), concatenates the payload
        byte, and drives \signame{ann\_core\_word}. The output tail is
        bit-for-bit equal to the input tail whenever the PI accepted
        it.}
    \label{fig:app-address-roundtrip}
\end{figure}
